Colleen Hoover Net Worth in 2023

Colleen Hoover has a net worth of $6 million. The American author, whose work is on new and young adult romance and fiction, made her wealth through her writing career. She has written and published over 24 books that made it to the New York Times Bestsellers list. Below is all to know about Colleen Hoover’s net worth and how she was able to make it.

The Sources of Colleen Hoover’s Net Worth of $6 Million

Colleen Hoover’s net worth is estimated at $6 million. While her career as an author gives her a lion’s share of her income, she has other sources from which she makes her money. Here are the sources of Colleen Hoover’s net worth.

The Author Earns From Her Books

Before Colleen Hoover became an author, she worked in various social work and teaching jobs. She began writing her first book, titled Slammed, in November 2011, and by January 2012, she published the book. After that, she wrote the continuation of her debut book, titled Point of Retreat, in 2012.

By August 2012, her debut book and the sequel reached #8 and #18 on the New York Times Best Seller. After the success of her debut book, she quit her social work and became a full-time writer. In December 2012, she published the novel titled Hopeless. The book topped #1 on the New York Times Best Seller in January 2013.

In July 2013, she published the companion of the book, titled Losing Hope. By April 2013, she published the third book, The Girl. Collen Hoover published a free novella, Finding Cinderella, in 2014, which includes the characters from Hopeless and Losing Hope. The author published Maybe Someday, Maybe Not, and Ugly Love in 2014.

In 2015, she co-authored the book Never Never with Tarryn Fisher. Collen moved on to release Confess and November 9 in 2015. She published the novels Too Late and It Ends With Us in 2016. She stated that the latter was the hardest book that she had ever written, and it ranked #1 on the New York Times Best Seller list in January 2022.

In 2017, Hoover published the book, Without Merit. She then published three books in 2018 – All Your Perfects, Verity, and Maybe Now. The year 2019 was not left out as she published a novel, Regretting You, and a novella, Finding Perfect. In 2020, she published Heart Bones and Layla.

Collen Hoover published a sequel to It Ends With Us titled It Starts With Us and another novel, Reminders of Him, in 2022. She also has other successful short stories that she has written, they are A Father’s Kiss, Saint, and The Dress. As of October 2022, she has reportedly sold over 20 million copies of her books, which amounts to quite a huge sum of money.

What is Colleen Hoover’s Best Book?

Colleen Hoover has written about twenty-four successful books and three short stories. Here is a glimpse of some of her best books:

1. It Ends With Us

It Ends With Us is absolutely the best of Colleen Hoover’s books, which was published in 2016. It has ranked #1 in the New York Times Bestseller for a long time after it was published. It has received over a million reviews on Goodreads.

The novel is all about a college graduate named Lily, who relocated to Boston and established a floral business. She met a neurosurgeon named Ryle, who had the rules of no dating but broke his rules when they met each other. Everything began to experience a turn after she learned about his dark secret and why he established the rule.

Having been caught in the web of a troubled relationship, she began to think about her first love and male protector, Atlas Corrigan. After Atlas re-emerged, all that Lily and Ryle had built together stood under threat.

Colleen Hoover translated the novel into twenty other languages, and it has sold over a million copies. She earns well from the sales of this book from both Amazon and other online book-selling platforms.

2. November 9

November 9 was published in 2015 and is one of the author’s books that made it to the New York Times Bestsellers list. It portrays a young woman, Fallon, who is trying to run away from a past that left both physical and emotional marks on her heart. A day before she was to relocate to another country, she met Ben, an aspiring novelist. They became close and spent Fallon’s last day together in L.A. Her life story became the inspiration that the novelist has always needed for his novel.

As time went by, the two would always meet on the same date every year. This continued till Fallon began to doubt if Ben had been truthful or made up things to look perfect.
