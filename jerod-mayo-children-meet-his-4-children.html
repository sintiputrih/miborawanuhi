Jerod Mayo Children: Meet His 4 Children – Jerod Mayo Sr. is currently serving as the head coach for the New England Patriots in the National Football League (NFL) while also maintaining a role as an American football coach. Prior to his coaching career, Mayo played as a linebacker for the Patriots for eight seasons.

Born on February 23, 1986, his journey began at the University of Tennessee, where he played college football and was drafted by the Patriots as the 10th overall pick in the 2008 NFL Draft.

He is the proud parent of a quartet of children, consisting of three daughters named Chya, Chyanne, and Chylo, alongside his son Jerod Jr. Each child brings their own unique personality and individuality, contributing to the vibrant tapestry of their family dynamics.

Known for his exceptional skills, Mayo earned various accolades during his playing career, including the Defensive Rookie of the Year title, leading the league in tackles in 2010, and contributing to the Patriots’ victory in Super Bowl XLIX.

Returning to the Patriots in 2019 as an assistant coach, Mayo’s coaching prowess led to his appointment as the head coach for the 2024 season. Born in Hampton, Virginia, Mayo’s early football achievements at Kecoughtan High School showcased his talent as a linebacker and running back. As a standout junior and senior, he received multiple All-District, All-Area, and All-Region honors.

Choosing the University of Tennessee over other notable schools, Jerod Mayo played for the Tennessee Volunteers football team from 2004 to 2007. His notable achievements included being named to the All-American second-team by Rivals.com and earning All-Southeastern Conference honors during his junior season.

In the 2008 NFL Draft, Jerod Mayo was selected by the New England Patriots in the first round. His rookie season saw him excel, leading to recognition as the AP Defensive Rookie of the Year. Mayo continued to make significant contributions in subsequent seasons, consistently showcasing his skills and earning Pro Bowl selections.

Mayo’s career faced challenges with injuries in the 2009 and 2013 seasons, but he remained a key player for the Patriots.

In 2011, he missed three games due to injury but still surpassed the 100-tackle mark. Mayo and the Patriots advanced to Super Bowl XLVI. Mayo recorded 11 tackles in the game, but the Patriots lost to the New York Giants 21-17.

For the fourth consecutive year, his teammates chose him as defensive co-captain. Mayo was fined $10,000 on November 16, 2012, for a late out-of-bounds hit against C. J. Spiller of the Buffalo Bills in Week 10. This resulted in an unnecessary roughness call.

Later that year, he was chosen for the 2013 Pro Bowl in recognition of his successful 2012 season. Mayo was placed on injured reserve on October 16 after tearing his pectoral muscle on October 13 during the Patriots’ comeback victory over the New Orleans Saints.

Jerod Mayo was placed on injured reserve on October 16 due to a torn patellar tendon sustained during a Week 6 game against the Buffalo Bills.

Without Mayo, the Patriots won Super Bowl XLIX, defeating the defending champion Seattle Seahawks 28-24.

Jerod Mayo’s playing time decreased in the 2015 season, as he fell behind Dont’a Hightower and Jamie Collins on the depth chart. Mayo was placed on injured reserve on January 19, 2016, just days before the AFC Championship game against the Denver Broncos, due to a shoulder injury.

On February 16, 2016, Mayo announced his retirement via Instagram and thanked the Patriots for the previous eight years.

Mayo has a well-known work ethic, earning him a reputation as a player who arrives early, stays late, watches film, practices hard, and works out intensely in the team’s weight room.

On March 27, 2019, Mayo was hired as the New England Patriots’ inside linebackers coach. On January 12, 2024, the New England Patriots named Mayo their 15th head coach in franchise history.
