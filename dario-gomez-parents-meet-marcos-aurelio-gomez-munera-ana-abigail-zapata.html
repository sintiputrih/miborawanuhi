Publish date: 2024-05-26

Dario Gomez is a Colombian composer and singer. In this article, we find out about Dario Gomez parents.

Born on 6 February 1951 in San Jerónimo, he stayed humble till his final gasp as he didn’t flaunt his millions and just purchased the things he could manage.

He began writing refrains at the age of 14 while working as a rancher because he really enjoyed singing ranchera music while picking coffee on Antioquia homesteads.

After the companion case, he relocated to Codiscos where he could focus only on music production.

He began serving as the music director in 1997 and worked in picaresque tone, vallenatos, and dance.

In 1985, Darío Gómez made his debut as a soloist and scored a new triumph when he performed “Decidelo (Decide it)”; then he released his first full length with the name of Así se le canta al despecho (So it is sung to spite).

On May 14, 2015, Gómez released the video of “me voy a casar (I’m going to marry)” next to the King of Popular Music Jhonny Rivera. On August 11, he would release the remix of No Hay Razón Para Odiarte (There’s no reason to hate you) in collaboration with Yelsid and Andy Rivera recording a reggaeton for the first time.

Gómez died at the age of 71 at Clínica Las Américas in the city of Medellín at 19:31 (COT), after being sent having fallen unconscious.

According to early accounts, he went unconscious at home and was transported right away to Clnica Las Américas in Medellin.

His condition wasn’t finalized because the medical professionals tried their best to revive him despite the fact that he didn’t give any obvious signs and the attempts failed.
