Tragic Incident: Firefighter Daniel Long’s Suicide Following Shooting of Ronda Firefighter Jenna Boles

A heartbreaking incident unfolded in North Wilkesboro, as firefighter Daniel Long tragically took his own life shortly after being involved in the accidental shooting of fellow Ronda Firefighter Jenna Boles. The incident has left the community reeling, shedding light on the profound impact of such tragedies on families, friends, and the firefighting community.

On August 10, a somber event transpired that claimed the lives of two young firefighters, casting a shadow over the tight-knit Wilkes County community. The Wilkes County Sheriff’s Office responded to a reported shooting on Caudill Road near North Wilkesboro at approximately 7:43 p.m.

Upon arriving at the scene, authorities found 21-year-old Daniel Long desperately attempting to render aid to 19-year-old Jenna Boles, who had sustained a gunshot wound. Long repeatedly expressed that the shooting had been an accident, but despite resolute efforts by deputies and medical personnel, Boles tragically succumbed to her injuries.

Jenna Boles was a committed firefighter with the Ronda Fire and Rescue department. Her dedication to serve her community reflects her character and the principles she held dear.

In a terrifying turn of events, Daniel Long, struck by the gravity of the situation, shot himself shortly afterwards. He was taken to the hospital but died as a result of his injuries.

The Wilkes County Sheriff’s Office, in collaboration with the State Bureau of Investigation, is aggressively investigating this very disturbing occurrence, looking for answers and insights into what happened.

Daniel Long, sometimes known as “Fridge,” was an important member of the North Wilkesboro Fire Department. Throughout his tenure, he demonstrated a strong commitment to the safety and well-being of North Wilkesboro people. In 2018, he joined the department as a junior fireman and displayed a dedication that reached beyond his immediate community.

The tragic deaths of Jenna Boles and Daniel Long have echoed throughout local fire departments and the whole region. The Ronda Fire Department conveyed their heartfelt condolences and requested the community to come together in support of the bereaved families of both firefighters.

The entire community is left coping with the unpleasant truths of mental health and the emotional toll that it may take in the aftermath of this heartbreaking occurrence. As the inquiry continues, there is a communal appeal for understanding, compassion, and open discourse to address the problems that firefighters and others may experience.
