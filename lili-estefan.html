Professional Career of Lili Estefan

Lili made her debut in the world of television in 1986 as one of the primary hosts of the venerable Sábado Gigante variety program in the Spanish language. She presided over the show for 12 arduous years. Then, in 1998, the now-54-year-old received an offer to co-host the Univision network’s celebrity gossip program El Gordo y la flaca. She earned the Spanish moniker La Flaca, which translates to The Skinny Girl in English, from that program.

Estefan is the niece of Emilio and Gloria Estefan, a famous couple. Early Years and Childhood of Alyssa Estefan

On March 20, 1967, in Santiago de Cuba, Lili Estefan was born. Liliana Del Carmen Estefan is her full name, but she prefers to be addressed as Lili in public. José Estefan is the name of her father. Her mother’s name is unknown.

Lili’s mother died when she was ten years old. After that, she moved to the United States with her family of three, which included her father, her brother, Juan Emilio, and herself. Her paternal uncle is the musician and producer Emilio Estefan, a Cuban American.

The television personality attended Miami’s Rockway Junior High after moving here. She later attended Miami Coral Park Senior High School, where she eventually received her diploma in 1984. She attended the New World School of the Arts and enrolled in the Performing and Visual Arts Center’s dual college program to earn a college degree.
